About

Morgan David Yadron recently graduated magna cum laude from the University of Arizona with a B.A. in Psychology and a minor in Philosophy. With a GPA of 3.7, his academic work reflects a deep commitment to exploring the intersections of mind, morality, behavior, and meaning. His primary interests include religious philosophy, ancient philosophy, deontology, and metaphysics—especially where psychological theory and philosophical inquiry converge. During his undergraduate years, Morgan was actively involved in campus life, contributing to both academic and extracurricular communities centered on dialogue, reflection, and service. He is currently pursuing graduate studies in philosophy at the Dominican School of Philosophy and Theology in Berkeley, California, where he aims to develop a robust understanding of the human condition through both analytic and historical lenses. Currently, Morgan is studying and engaging in the critical concepts of free will and determinism, particularly within a divine and metaphysical approach. Thank you for stopping by, and feel free to browse through the CV, essays, talks, and updates on my work.
